# Theme D — What fits OUR scale and product constraint

Reality check on where "intent" can live in a 4.3M-param cleanup + classical-tracer vectorizer, given the print-fidelity constraint (sharpen what is there, never confidently invent what is not). Build-order recommendation at the end.

Register: plain changelog. Every load-bearing claim is cited; verdicts from the adversarial verification pass are folded in. Read this document alone to make the roadmap call.

---

## 1. Executive summary

The intent-mechanism ladder splits cleanly into what is **proven at our parameter scale (~4–20M)** and what is not, and separately into what is **compatible with never-invent** and what structurally fights it.

- **Explicit conditioning (FiLM / spatial-FiLM / joint semantic loss) is proven at our exact scale and is cheap.** The modulation itself costs ~2 params per feature map; a segmentation-map-driven condition net is 4 layers of 1×1 conv; a frozen-recognizer semantic loss was demonstrated on a ~1M-param denoiser. This is the rung that fits.
- **The knowledge source is the real cost, not the injection.** Off-the-shelf frozen CLIP embeddings were shown to *fail* at improving restoration; making foundation features useful required a trained controller (~125M params) far larger than the restoration net. Cheap conditioning requires a cheap, in-domain condition signal — for us that is our own 16-class region-label head, not a bolted-on foundation model.
- **Gains are strongly degradation-specific.** Semantic guidance pays off most where degradation destroys recognizable structure (our pixelated-star case), and near-zero for mild noise/blur. None of the cited results are in-domain (all natural photos); transfer to flat graphic art is by mechanism/analogy, not demonstrated.
- **Generative priors (diffusion / DiffBIR) structurally invent.** The perception-distortion tradeoff makes this provably unavoidable: you cannot have both maximal fidelity and maximal perceptual "cleanness" past the bound. Diffusion is usable only clamped, per-region, defaulted toward fidelity — and it is not a small-net technique.
- **The competitor puts intent in a classical Stage-2 geometry layer, not a generative prior.** Vectorizer.ai fits parametric primitives (stars N=3–6, circles, rounded rects) and models symmetry — which is exactly the invention our product forbids. That makes a *fidelity gate* our differentiator and confirms primitive fitting is viable engineering; the risk is entirely in the acceptance policy.
- **Bounding invention is a gate/policy problem layered on the model, not a single objective.** Efficient-RANSAC acceptance gates, SelectiveNet abstention, and uncertainty-driven pass-through are the transplantable guardrails; the prepress norm (PitStop: report-then-approve, auditable) reinforces surfacing substitutions rather than baking them in.

Build order: harden the region-label head → spatial-FiLM self-conditioning → Stage-2 gated primitive fitting → per-region abstention/uncertainty → (only if justified) clamped generative refinement.

## 2. Findings by sub-topic

### 2.1 Explicit conditioning is cheap AND proven at our scale

The injection mechanism is genuinely near-free in parameters:

- **FiLM** adds two parameters per modulated feature map (one scale γ, one shift β): `FiLM(F | γ,β) = γ·F + β`. The paper states it is "a scalable and computationally efficient conditioning method" whose cost "does not scale with the image resolution." It roughly halved CLEVR error (95.5%→97.7%, i.e. 4.5%→2.3%) and gave >50% larger fine-tune gains than competitors in the low-data (18k-sample) CLEVR-Humans regime — a **data-efficiency** result, directly on-point for a data-constrained net ([Perez et al. 2017, FiLM](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1709.07871)). *Verified CONFIRMED.* Caveat surfaced in verification: "2 params, essentially free" counts only the modulation, **not** the generator network that predicts γ/β. In our pipeline that generator is the recognizer + a small MLP — cheap only if the recognizer is cheap (see 2.2).

- **Spatial FiLM / SFT-GAN** drives modulation *spatially* from a semantic segmentation probability map via a condition net that is only 4 layers of 1×1 conv, in a ~16-resblock SR net: `SFT(F | γ,β) = γ⊙F + β`. Critically, when a region's class is unknown it is labelled "background," the net emits default γ/β and **degenerates to the unconditioned baseline rather than inventing wrong-class texture** ([Wang et al. 2018, SFT-GAN](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1804.02815)). *Verified CONFIRMED, with two corrections to the framing:* (1) the graceful-fallback target is SRGAN — itself a texture-hallucinating GAN — so the guarantee is "no *wrong-class* texture," **not** "no invention." Our L1+region-CE UNet is already more conservative than that fallback and inherits the good property (neutral β/γ on unknown regions) **only if we keep the reconstruction loss and do not bolt on a GAN.** (2) Domain is outdoor natural texture (sky, grass, fur), not graphic art; the print-fidelity match is by mechanism, not demonstrated in-domain.

- **Joint semantic loss at ~1M params (our exact scale).** A ~1,026k-param three-scale denoiser trained jointly with a **frozen** VGG-16 + DeepLab via a semantic loss gained only +~0.07 dB PSNR over DnCNN (Kodak σ=35: 30.83 vs 30.76), but the semantic gradient concentrated on "texture-rich regions that are prone to suffer from oversmoothing," and downstream task accuracy jumped massively (ILSVRC top-1 σ=30: 44.4%→67.1%; VOC mIoU: 43.43→57.88) ([Liu et al. 2018](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1809.01826)). *Verified CONFIRMED at our parameter regime, with two scope caveats:* (1) the "massive jump" numbers are **downstream task accuracy on noisy inputs** (a robustness result), not an image-fidelity metric like SSIM/LPIPS — the "helps fidelity more than PSNR" gloss is inference, not measured. (2) The mechanism credited (preserving fine stochastic texture like fur/feathers that L2 oversmooths) has little analogue in flat-fill graphic art, and the same recognizer prior that preserves a genuine fine-line stroke is what could nudge a deliberately-asymmetric star toward a learned generic one.

**Read for us:** injecting "this region is a star / this is type" into the 4.3M UNet via spatial FiLM costs almost nothing in parameters, is demonstrated at our scale, and has a built-in graceful-degradation property that aligns with never-invent — *provided the condition signal is our own in-domain region head and the loss stays reconstruction-anchored.*

### 2.2 The knowledge source is not free — off-the-shelf foundation features fail

The expensive half of conditioning is producing a *useful* condition signal:

- **DA-CLIP:** off-the-shelf frozen CLIP embeddings **fail** to substantially improve unified restoration (degradation-classification 26.7% with raw OpenAI CLIP vs 99.2% with the trained controller). Making it work required a trained image **controller** — a copy of the CLIP image encoder (~125.2M params, base CLIP kept frozen), injected via cross-attention only in the U-Net bottom blocks — which **dwarfs** the ~36–49M restoration net (+3.37 dB PSNR on unified restoration) ([Luo et al. 2023, DA-CLIP](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2310.01018)). *Verified CONFIRMED with one correction:* it is not "a full copy of CLIP ViT-B/32" as a clone — it is a trained copy of the CLIP *image encoder* with zero-initialised connections, base CLIP frozen. Regime is a ~36M diffusion U-Net on natural images; transfer to a 4.3M feed-forward net on graphic art is not established.

- **SAM-prior distillation:** semantic priors from a large recognizer (SAM) can be **distilled into a lightweight restoration net at training time only** — "our method only uses SAM at the training stage and preserves the same inference efficiency" — so no big encoder at test time. But the gain is strongly task-dependent: large for structured degradations (deraining +1.22 to +1.28 dB) and near-zero for denoise/deblur (+0.11 dB) ([Wang et al. 2024, SAM priors](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2403.16368)). *Verified CONFIRMED* (one transcription error in the raw notes — RCDNet Rain200L baseline is 39.04, not 36.09; 39.04→40.26 = the reported +1.22, so the figure is right). Regime is photographic; the paper cannot tell you whether pixelated-vector-render cleanup patterns like the "structured" bucket or the "near-zero" bucket.

- **Relational KD** (supporting, not decision-critical): a small student can match or exceed its teacher when distilled on relational structure — ResNet18 student 82.50% R@1 vs ResNet50 teacher 77.17% on Cars196 — general evidence that recognizer "intent" compresses into a small net, though in classification/metric-learning, not restoration ([Park et al. 2019, RKD](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1904.05068)).

**Read for us:** we already train a 16-class region-label head end-to-end on in-domain synthetic data. That is exactly the "cheap, in-domain, trained condition signal" DA-CLIP shows you need — and it is free of the 125M encoder tax. The cleanest fit is **self-conditioning: feed our own region logits back as spatial-FiLM modulation**, plus optionally training-time distillation if we ever want a stronger prior without test-time cost.

### 2.3 Generative priors structurally invent — usable only clamped

- **Perception-distortion tradeoff (theory backbone):** no estimator can simultaneously achieve arbitrarily low distortion (fidelity, under *any* full-reference measure) and high perceptual quality; P(D) is monotonically non-increasing. Perceptual quality equals ½·d_TV(p_X, p_X̂)+½ — proportional to the total-variation distance between your outputs' distribution and the clean-image distribution — and GANs "provide a principled way to approach the perception-distortion bound" ([Blau & Michaeli 2018](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1711.06077)). Direct read: a distortion-anchored (L1 + region-CE) cleanup on the low-distortion side is the fidelity-safe regime; pushing perceptual "cleanness" (idealizing a wonky star) necessarily moves along the curve *away* from fidelity. **A single objective cannot do both past the bound — you need an explicit knob, not a cleverer loss.**

- **DiffBIR** is the "restore vs. redraw, per region, on a dial" pattern in the wild: Stage 1 removes degradation for high-fidelity output; Stage 2 (IRControlNet) "generate[s] realistic details that weren't present in the original degraded input," with an inference-time, retraining-free region-adaptive guidance scale trading realness against fidelity ([Lin et al. 2023, DiffBIR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.15070)). The generative stage **by construction synthesizes absent content** — so if adopted it must be gated per-region and clamped toward fidelity for logo/type regions. It is also not a small-net technique.

**Read for us:** diffusion is the wrong default for print fidelity and the wrong scale for us. Its one useful export is the *architecture pattern* — fidelity stage + optional clamped refinement on a per-region dial — which we can adopt without the generative model itself.

### 2.4 The competitor puts intent in classical Stage-2 geometry

- Vectorizer.ai ships **parametric primitive fitting** as a first-class stage: fully parameterized circles, ellipses, rounded rectangles, isosceles triangles, **stars (N=3,4,5,6)**, and D-shapes, gated to fire "where possible" for "a perfect fit and unmatched consistency"; default output "Flatten" converts fitted shapes back to ordinary curves (fitting is internal, optionally surfaced) ([API output options](https://vectorizer.ai/api/outputOptions)).
- It explicitly **detects and models mirror AND rotational symmetry** — "to produce more accurate and more consistent results" — achieved via classical geometry on top of a segmentation model, **not** a generative prior ([homepage](https://vectorizer.ai/)).
- Architecture is a hybrid of from-scratch deep-learning networks **plus** classical algorithms on a proprietary dataset — structurally the same DL+classical split our two-stage pipeline uses.
- Neither Vectorizer.ai, its predecessor Vector Magic, nor vtracer supports true gradient meshes — all emit flat/solid fills — so **our gradient gap is not a competitive disadvantage.**
- The open no-intent floor (vtracer: boundary tracing + spline fitting, zero primitive/symmetry modeling, documented defaults) is the ~1.28 deltaE capability our engine already matches ([vtracer config](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/visioncortex/vtracer/master/cmdapp/src/config.rs)).

**Read for us:** the market leader's intent layer is exactly the move our product forbids — their star-fitting and symmetry "correction" would snap a deliberately-asymmetric customer star to a generic one. Their FAQ even says the AI "makes sensible guesses when the pixels are a mess." A **fidelity gate they do not offer is our differentiation**, and primitive fitting at Stage 2 is proven-viable engineering — the entire risk sits in the acceptance policy, not the fitting math.

### 2.5 Guardrails that transplant cleanly (bounding invention is a gate problem)

- **Efficient-RANSAC acceptance gate** — the canonical "only substitute when the fit is genuinely supported" pattern. A candidate primitive scores `σ_P(ψ)=|P_ψ|` over points within a distance band ε *and* normal deviation <α, taking the largest connected component, and is accepted only when the probability that no better candidate was overlooked exceeds p_t (they use 99%), *and* it clears a minimum-support floor τ ([Schnabel et al., Efficient RANSAC](https://www.hinkali.com/Education/PointCloud.pdf)). Template for Stage-2: replace the freeform Schneider bezier path with a primitive **only when** the primitive's residual/support beats the freeform fit by a margin **and** clears a statistical-confidence floor — a two-sided gate, not a single tolerance.
- **SelectiveNet** — a trainable, calibratable abstention head: a sigmoid selection head g(x); predict iff g(x)≥τ (τ=0.5 default), else abstain; τ post-hoc set to the 100·(1−c) percentile of g on validation to hit a target coverage c. On CIFAR-10, selective risk drops from 6.79% at full coverage to 0.32% at 70% coverage ([Geifman & El-Yaniv 2019](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1901.09192)). Maps to a per-region "restore vs. leave-alone" head with an owner-tunable aggression dial.
- **Aleatoric/epistemic uncertainty** (supporting) — a learned per-pixel variance gives calibrated confidence at no extra labels; epistemic uncertainty is the principled OOD flag for "content type I haven't learned" (our typography/gradient gaps), which should default to pass-through ([Kendall & Gal 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1703.04977)).
- **Prepress norm (PitStop)** — the industry our customers already trust institutionalizes report-then-fix: preflight generates a report with clickable jump-points, fixes apply via explicit auditable Action Lists, Certified PDF tracks versions so every change is logged and reversible ([Enfocus PitStop](https://www.enfocus.com/en/pitstop-pro)). Argues for surfacing proposed substitutions (esp. primitive snaps) as reviewable/undoable diffs rather than baking them in.

## 3. Recommendations

| Recommendation | Confidence + why | Cheapest confirming experiment (cost) |
|---|---|---|
| Make intent explicit via **spatial-FiLM self-conditioning**: feed the existing 16-class region-label logits back as γ/β modulation into the RGB-repair decoder. | High — mechanism proven at our exact ~1M-param scale, ~free in params, in-domain signal avoids the DA-CLIP encoder tax; graceful-degradation property aligns with never-invent. | Add SFT layers to the decoder, retrain on current 927k set, compare deltaE on the 24-image bench (~1 training run, hours of GPU). |
| **Do NOT bolt on a foundation encoder (CLIP/SAM) at inference.** If a stronger prior is wanted, distill it at training time only. | High — DA-CLIP shows off-the-shelf frozen CLIP *fails* and the working controller (~125M) dwarfs a 4.3M net; SAM distillation keeps inference cost identical. | N/A up front; if pursued, one distillation run vs. baseline on the bench (one extra training run). |
| **Keep cleanup distortion-anchored (L1 + region-CE); do not add a GAN/adversarial term.** | High — perception-distortion theorem makes fidelity vs. perceptual-cleanness provably exclusive past the bound; adversarial loss moves you off the fidelity side. | No experiment needed; this is a theory-backed constraint. Optionally confirm by measuring deltaE regression if a GAN term is ever trialed. |
| Put **geometric intent in Stage-2 as gated primitive fitting** (star/circle/rounded-rect), never as an unconditional snap. | High — competitor proves primitive fitting is viable; the only risk is the acceptance policy, which is the differentiator. | Implement one primitive (circle) with an Efficient-RANSAC-style two-sided gate; measure on synthetic asymmetric-vs-regular test shapes (days of Rust work). |
| Gate every substitution with a **two-sided acceptance test**: primitive residual/support must beat freeform Schneider fit by a margin AND clear a confidence floor (p_t≈99%). | High — canonical, well-specified pattern; directly enforces never-invent at the geometry layer. | Unit-test the gate on a held set of deliberately-asymmetric shapes: assert no snap occurs (hours, part of the fitting task). |
| Add a **per-region abstention / restore-vs-leave-alone head** (SelectiveNet-style) with an owner-tunable coverage dial; default OOD regions (type, gradients) to pass-through. | Medium — proven in classification, not restoration; transfer to per-region cleanup is by analogy. Value is high for our known content gaps. | Add a selection head + percentile-calibrated threshold, measure whether abstained regions correlate with known-hard content (one training run + eval). |
| **Surface proposed primitive substitutions as reviewable/undoable diffs**, not silent auto-corrections, matching the PitStop report-then-approve norm. | Medium — product/UX inference from prepress convention, not a benchmarked ML result, but matches the target users' existing mental model. | Prototype a before/after diff view on 10 real NAS logos; get operator (Alan/Ipek) reaction (a day of UI work). |
| **Do not adopt diffusion as the cleanup default**; if generative refinement is ever added, use DiffBIR's clamped per-region guidance defaulted near-zero for logo/type. | Medium-high — theory + product constraint both point away from generative default; not a small-net technique, so scale-mismatched for us now. | Defer; revisit only if explicit-conditioning + Stage-2 fitting plateau above target deltaE. |

## 4. Open questions

1. **Domain transfer is unproven.** Every quantitative result cited (FiLM, SFT-GAN, joint semantic loss, SAM/CLIP) is on natural photographs or synthetic reasoning scenes — none on flat graphic art, logos, wordmarks, or typography. The mechanism transfers in principle; the *magnitude* does not. Our degradation (pixelation/compression of vector renders) could pattern like the high-gain "structured" bucket or the near-zero bucket; the literature cannot say which. Only an in-domain ablation on our 24-image bench (and ideally NAS-mined tiles) settles it.
2. **Which bucket is our degradation?** SAM-prior gains ranged +1.28 dB (deraining) to +0.11 dB (denoise). We do not know where "restore a pixelated star render" falls. Cheapest resolution: the spatial-FiLM ablation above — if region-conditioning barely moves deltaE, we are in the low-gain bucket and should invest in Stage-2 geometry instead.
3. **Does Stage-1 discard the anti-aliasing sub-pixel information Stage-2 needs?** Vectorizer.ai places boundaries "according to the anti-aliasing pixel values" and recovers sub-pixel features; our 256px UNet may throw this away before the tracer sees it. Not addressed by any cited restoration paper — needs an internal probe of what edge information survives cleanup.
4. **Calibration of the abstention/uncertainty dial on our data.** SelectiveNet's coverage calibration and Kendall-Gal's epistemic OOD flag are demonstrated on classification; whether the epistemic signal reliably fires on our specific gaps (typography, fineline, gradients) rather than on benign hard edges is untested and needs a labeled OOD probe set.
5. **Where does the fidelity gate's threshold sit for real customer intent?** The two-sided acceptance gate has a margin and a confidence floor, but the *right* values that snap a sloppy-scan circle while sparing a deliberately-asymmetric brand star are an empirical, per-customer question — likely needs the report-then-approve UX (rec. 7) to gather ground truth rather than a fixed constant.
