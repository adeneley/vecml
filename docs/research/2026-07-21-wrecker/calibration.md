# Theme C — Calibrating the wrecker against our real files

Date: 2026-07-21
Component under study: `src/vecml/degrade/pipeline.py` + `src/vecml/degrade/wreck.py`
Framing (owner's, correct): "We are training a model to reverse our very specific wrecker. If our wrecker isn't right, nothing is right." The degradation model IS the task definition, so it should be *measured* against the real corpus, not hand-tuned.

## 1. Executive summary

The current wrecker maps one `severity` scalar onto fixed op ranges: `jpeg_cycle` (quality lerped 92→8, at most 2 passes, almost always placed last), a single-order chain of structural ops, isotropic-only Gaussian blur (`sigmaX==sigmaY`, 0.4–3.5), homoscedastic additive Gaussian noise (std 3–40), and no sinc/ringing, no Poisson/signal-dependent noise, no scan/halftone model, no op-order shuffling. It is a plausible but *unmeasured* guess.

This report gives a concrete calibration protocol built on the ~400 harvested real damaged-original / clean-PRINT pairs:

1. **Profile the real corpus per-file** with off-the-shelf, mostly training-free forensic estimators (JPEG quantization tables, double-JPEG detectors, wavelet noise sigma, resampling periodicity) to turn each wrecker parameter from a guess into a measured distribution.
2. **Measure the domain gap** with a Classifier Two-Sample Test (C2ST) / proxy-A-distance as the headline number, on content-controlled paired data, cross-checked with KID (not FID) and high-frequency spectral statistics.
3. **Exploit the unaligned pairs directly** (raster-damaged vs vector-clean) using a contextual/CoBi loss and a train-synthetic/test-real gap diagnostic, instead of chasing pixel registration.
4. **Close the loop**: sample the measured distributions in wrecker v2, add the missing ops (anisotropic + motion blur, Poisson noise, sinc/ringing, scan/ISP noise, order shuffle, multi-generation + misaligned JPEG), and use a patch discriminator to down-weight synthetic samples that still read as unreal.

The single biggest correction to the raw research: **KernelGAN is a plausible blur *probe*, not a drop-in blur oracle for our regime** — it estimates one confounded SR-resampling kernel per natural photo and cannot separate a blur PSF from JPEG blocking / halftone / upsample staircase, which is exactly the composite damage that dominates our files. Adopt the anisotropy gap it exposes; source the kernels analytically (BSRGAN/Real-ESRGAN style) instead.

## 2. Findings by sub-topic

### 2.1 Per-file forensic profiling of the real corpus (measure, don't guess)

**JPEG quality is recoverable from the file's quantization tables — CONFIRMED (exact for standard tables, approximate otherwise).** Quality can be estimated deterministically from the quant tables: `qvalue = quantval[2]+quantval[53]` (luminance) `+ quantval[0]+quantval[63]` (chrominance), plus the total table sum, matched against the IJG `hash[101]`/`sums[101]` arrays to yield `quality=i+1` ([ImageMagick `JPEGSetImageQuality`](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/ImageMagick/ImageMagick/main/coders/jpeg.c), exposed as `identify -format '%Q'`). Tables are readable in pure Python via Pillow's `JpegImageFile.quantization` dict ([JPEG / IJG scaling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JPEG)). This is header parsing, so it is content-independent and works on logos/scans exactly as on photos. Caveat: `%Q` is exact only for standard IJG-scaled tables; Photoshop Save-for-Web, mozjpeg, and Office re-encoders carry non-standard tables and yield an approximation. Practical consequence: bucket `%Q` coarsely, and for high-fidelity calibration store the raw 64-entry tables themselves. This directly replaces the wrecker's hardcoded `quality = lerp(92, 8, severity)`.

**Compression-generation count is measurable — CONFIRMED (tooling).** Double/misaligned JPEG detectors (ADQ1/2/3, NADQ) in the pure-Python [pyIFD](https://github.com/EldritchJS/pyIFD) toolbox expose aligned double-quantization traces; misaligned re-save (screenshot → re-JPEG) is the NADQ non-aligned case. This lets us replace the wrecker's `passes = 2 if severity > 0.66 else 1` rule with a corpus-calibrated pass-count distribution — real emailed/WhatsApped/screenshotted files are often 3+ generations, and the misaligned-grid chain type is one the wrecker never simulates. pyIFD bundles ~14 estimators (ADQ/NADQ, NOI noise maps, BLK/DCT blocking strength, CFA, ELA/GHOST) as one dependency covering most of the wrecker's parameter space; most output a heat-map reducible to a scalar per file.

**Noise sigma has a one-call estimator — CONFIRMED.** [`skimage.restoration.estimate_sigma`](https://scikit-image.org/docs/stable/api/skimage.restoration.html) is a robust wavelet (Donoho–Johnstone MAD) estimator, per-channel via `channel_axis`, no training. It calibrates the additive-noise leg (`gaussian_noise`, std 3–40). Scope caveat: it models additive white Gaussian noise from finest-scale wavelet detail coefficients; on flat graphic art with hard edges + JPEG blocking + halftone, those coefficients are dominated by signal/artefact structure, so it *over-reads* on sharp-edged graphics. Use it as a calibration probe for the additive-noise leg only, not a total damage-magnitude oracle — and measure it on flat regions where possible.

**Blur kernel via KernelGAN — CONFIRMED as tool, REFUTED as drop-in for our regime (corrected).** KernelGAN trains a per-image internal GAN from scratch (no pretrained model, no reference) to estimate a free-form non-parametric ~13×13 kernel; generator is a 6-layer bias-free *linear* conv net `G_structure=[7,5,3,1,1,1]`, discriminator a fully-convolutional patch net; by default only sum-to-one (0.5) and boundary (0.5) regularizers are active plus a dominant bicubic anchor (5.0), with the sparse/centralized terms weighted 0 ([configs.py](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sefibk/KernelGAN/master/configs.py), [paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.06581)). It costs ~61 s/image on a V100 (~102 s on K80), independent of image size, so it is inherently an *offline profiling* tool, not an online wrecker op.

*Correction (the raw claim that KernelGAN-estimated kernels should replace the blur op was REFUTED):* KernelGAN estimates one blind-SR *downsampling* kernel per image under a natural-image cross-scale patch-recurrence prior, validated on photographs (DIV2KRK). In our regime it (a) recovers a *confounded* kernel — it explains the whole low-res image as one latent-HR-through-one-kernel and cannot disentangle a clean blur PSF from JPEG blocking, halftone/moire, palette crush, or nearest/bicubic upsample staircase, which is exactly our composite damage; (b) is untested on flat graphic art / typography; and (c) contradicts standard *forward*-degradation practice — Real-ESRGAN/BSRGAN deliberately sample analytic anisotropic kernels rather than per-image estimated ones precisely because estimated kernels are noisy and not a controllable sampling bank. **Adopt the gap it exposes (the current op is isotropic-only and cannot represent real anisotropy), reject the acquisition method.** Source anisotropic/motion/defocus kernels analytically (see 2.4). If KernelGAN is run at all, treat its output as a *candidate anisotropy prior to validate against the real pairs*, and run it only on the cleaner near-single-resample subset of the corpus.

**Resampling factor via Popescu–Farid EM periodicity — decision-non-critical, use as a prior only.** Interpolation leaves periodic pixel correlations that show as DFT peaks in the EM probability map ([Popescu–Farid](https://hfarid.org/downloads/publications/sp05.pdf)). Hard ambiguity to respect: the factor is *not* uniquely recoverable (e.g. 3/4 vs 5/4 alias identically, integer downsampling leaves no periodicity), and in a chain only the *last* resample dominates the spectrum. Implication: fit a resample-factor *prior*, model the final resample step distinctly, and do not try to recover exact per-file factors. The upsampled-favicon case is partly covered by the existing `downscale_upscale` op, but that op only shrinks-then-enlarges; real favicons are *up*-sampled from a tiny source with no prior downscale, which the current op does not represent.

### 2.2 Measuring the domain gap: is the wrecker right?

**Headline metric — Classifier Two-Sample Test (C2ST) / proxy-A-distance.** Train a binary classifier on real-damaged vs synthetically-damaged images; held-out accuracy near 0.5 means the distributions are indistinguishable (wrecker matched), near 1.0 means a large gap ([C2ST](https://arxiv.org/abs/1610.06545)). Test statistic `t = 2·n_te·(p̂ − 0.5)`, `Z = sqrt(n_te)·(2p̂ − 1) ~ N(0,1)`, `p = 2·Φ(−|Z|)`, 50/50 split. The same quantity appears in domain adaptation as proxy-A-distance `= 2(1 − 2ε)` (ε = domain-classifier error), which is what [DANN](https://arxiv.org/abs/1505.07818) minimizes — track this single number across wrecker versions as the north star.

**The dominant trap (specific to this task): the classifier discriminates on CONTENT, not degradation.** Clean SVG art vs real customer logos differ in content, so C2ST will report a huge gap even with a perfect wrecker. Mandatory mitigations: (a) build the synthetic set by degrading the *same* clean sources whose real-damaged counterparts we hold (the ~400 pairs make this feasible), so only the degradation differs; (b) run the test on degradation *residuals* (wrecked − clean) rather than raw images; (c) always run a positive control (feed an obviously-wrong synthetic set and confirm accuracy → 1.0) to prove the classifier is not merely under-powered. Documented failure mode: an under-capacity classifier reports ~0.5 ("matched") even when distributions differ, and power collapses in high dimensions — a "matched" result is only trustworthy after the positive control passes.

**Feature-space distance: use KID, not FID.** FID is treacherous here for three independent reasons. (1) It is dangerously sensitive to low-level image handling — merely changing the resize implementation (OpenCV/PyTorch/TF bicubic instead of correct PIL bicubic) shifts FID by ≥6 points, and JPEG format alone can flip rankings ([clean-fid](https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.11222)). Since the wrecker's whole job is resize+JPEG, naive FID measures our preprocessing, not the degradation match. (2) Its InceptionV3 features are ImageNet-tuned and unreliable for logos/line-art/typography — our exact target — and can be moved without any quality change ([Kynkäänniemi et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.06026)). (3) FID is biased and sample-size-dependent; with only ~400 pairs it is systematically inflated. [KID](https://arxiv.org/abs/1801.01401) (unbiased squared-MMD with a polynomial kernel) is the recommended small-sample distance. Run everything under one frozen, documented resize+compression path (clean-fid style) for both real and synthetic sets, and consider features from a network adapted to line-art (or the vectorizer's own encoder) rather than ImageNet Inception.

**Cheap content-robust cross-check: high-frequency spectral statistics.** Real-vs-synthetic degradation mismatch concentrates in the *high*-frequency band; low frequencies are largely preserved by downsampling ([Frequency Separation, AIM 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1911.07850)). Comparing high-pass residuals / power spectra of real-damaged vs synthetic-damaged sets is a cheap check that is less content-sensitive than raw pixels.

### 2.3 Using the unaligned pairs (raster-damaged vs vector-clean)

**The domain gap is large and directly measurable as a train/test delta.** The same SR model scored ~9.4 dB PSNR / 0.20 LPIPS *better* on bicubic-degraded versions of images than on the real degradation of the same scenes ([DRealSR, Table 6](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2008.01928)); real pairs beat bicubic-synthetic training by ~1.0 dB ([RealSR](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1904.00523)) — a number that backs the owner's framing. The equivalent diagnostic for us: train the cleanup model on wrecker output, then measure its end-to-end deltaE on (i) held-out synthetic pairs vs (ii) the ~400 real pairs. A large gap = wrecker under-covers reality; shrinking that gap is the calibration objective.

**Don't chase pixel alignment.** The published capture rigs still need iterative affine+luminance fits *plus manual patch rejection* and report no residual-error metric ([DRealSR](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2008.01928)); our raster-vs-vector pairs are worse. Instead use a deformation-tolerant loss/metric: the [Contextual Loss](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1803.02077) treats an image as an unordered set of VGG features and ignores position (removes noise where L1 gives "a drastically blurred image"), and [Contextual Bilateral (CoBi)](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/1905.05169) adds a spatial-distance penalty, tolerating 40–80 px shifts and beating pixel L1/L2 which "generate blurred images" on misaligned data. Caveat: the plain Contextual Loss paper reports only qualitative results and gives no maximum-tolerable-misalignment bound. Use CoBi (RGB, n≈10–15) + λ·CoBi-VGG for both training on and scoring against the unaligned real clean-PRINT references.

**No-ground-truth eval is legitimate.** State-of-the-art real-world SR validates on real images with no-reference NIQE + visual inspection and does *not* fine-tune on real data ([Real-ESRGAN](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2107.10833)) — and its documented failures (twisted lines from aliasing, GAN artefacts on fineline) mirror the vectorizer's typography weakness, confirming this failure class comes from degradation coverage.

### 2.4 Closing the loop: measurements → wrecker v2 priors

**Sample measured distributions, not a single severity scalar.** The wrecker currently derives every op range from one `severity`. Replace the JPEG quality lerp with sampling from the measured `%Q` histogram (2.1), the pass count with the measured ADQ/NADQ generation distribution, and the noise std with the measured `estimate_sigma` distribution.

**Calibrate from the corpus, not by hand — two validated mechanisms.** (1) The NTIRE-2020-winning [Impressionism](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2005.01996) recipe estimates real blur kernels and *harvests real noise patches* (filtered to low-variance) and injects them into clean renders — the noise-patch harvest is directly portable and sidesteps the "is our Gaussian noise the right shape" question entirely. (2) [DASR](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/abs/2004.01178) trains a patch discriminator separating synthetic-degraded from real-degraded patches; it both *diagnoses* the gap (output histograms) and *down-weights* unrealistic synthetic samples during training (improved LPIPS 0.378→0.359). This is a concrete closed loop: discriminator on wrecked-synthetic vs the ~400 real patches, use its score to rebalance the wrecker.

**Concrete op deltas for wrecker v2** (against `wreck.py` as it stands), ordered by expected coverage gain:

- **Multi-generation + misaligned JPEG.** Raise the pass cap above 2, sample pass count from the measured distribution, and add a misaligned-grid re-save variant (shift a few px between passes) to model screenshot→re-JPEG (the NADQ case). Also vary chroma subsampling (4:2:0 vs 4:4:4), currently fixed by Pillow defaults.
- **Anisotropic + motion + defocus blur.** The current `gaussian_blur` is isotropic-only (`sigmaX==sigmaY`). Add independent `sigmaX`/`sigmaY` + rotation angle, a random motion-blur kernel, and an optional defocus-disc kernel — the [Real-ESRGAN](https://github.com/xinntao/Real-ESRGAN/blob/master/options/finetune_realesrgan_x4plus.yml)/[BSRGAN](https://github.com/cszn/BSRGAN/blob/main/utils/utils_blindsr.py) synthetic-kernel bank (six families: iso/aniso/generalized/plateau), sourced analytically, not via KernelGAN.
- **Sinc / ringing filter.** Real-ESRGAN applies a final sinc with prob 0.8 to model ringing; the wrecker has no sinc term (its `unsharp_halo` is a different, sharpening-halo artefact).
- **Poisson / signal-dependent noise + a scan/camera-ISP noise op.** Current `gaussian_noise` is homoscedastic additive only. Add Poisson noise (Real-ESRGAN `poisson_scale_range`) and a camera-ISP/scan-noise op (BSRGAN) to cover photographed cards and scanner sensor noise.
- **Op-order shuffle + second-order chain.** The wrecker always places JPEG last and never permutes structural ops. BSRGAN randomly shuffles op order (last resize stays last); Real-ESRGAN runs the whole blur→resize→noise→JPEG chain *twice*. Both cover the multi-generation histories (WhatsApp/screenshot/re-save) the single-order fixed-JPEG-last recipe undersamples.
- **Pure up-sample (favicon) branch.** Add an up-sample-from-tiny op (source << 256 px, nearest/bicubic enlarge) distinct from `downscale_upscale`, to model enlarged small web logos with no prior downscale step.
- **Scan-specific ops (lower priority, gated by corpus prevalence):** halftone/moire pattern, paper-texture overlay, small skew/rotation. Add only if the corpus profiling shows scans are a meaningful fraction of intake.

## 4. Open questions

- **Corpus composition.** What fraction of the ~400 pairs are each intake type (upsampled favicon, JPEG chain, letterhead scan, Office re-extract, photographed card, flattened PDF)? The forensic profiler (rec #1) answers this and sets the wrecker's op-mix priors; scan-specific ops are only worth adding if scans are a meaningful share.
- **Non-standard JPEG tables.** How large is the Photoshop/Office fraction where `%Q` is only approximate? If large, calibration should sample raw quant tables, not the collapsed `%Q`.
- **Which encoder feature space for KID/C2ST?** ImageNet Inception is a poor fit for logos/line-art; is the vectorizer's own encoder (or a line-art-adapted net) available and better? Needs a small ablation.
- **CoBi misalignment ceiling.** No published maximum-tolerable-shift bound exists; our raster-vs-vector pairs may exceed it. Needs an empirical check on a few deliberately-shifted pairs before trusting CoBi scores.
- **Vector-clean vs raster-clean reference.** The clean side is a vector PDF; rasterizing it introduces its own resample. Which rasterization DPI/filter makes the clean reference a fair target for the damaged raster? Affects every paired metric.
- **How to combine per-op measured distributions into a joint recipe.** Profiling gives marginals (blur, noise, JPEG independently); real files have correlated histories (a WhatsApp chain co-occurs with specific quality + generation counts). Does sampling marginals independently under-cover, and is the DASR discriminator (rec #8) enough to catch that?
