# THEME 1 — FUEL: Census of Minable Born-Vector Datasets

Deep-research synthesis, 2026-07-20. Scope: every born-vector (and adjacent) data source that could feed the Stage-1 cleanup UNet and Stage-2 tracer, ranked by how well it attacks our three measured gaps — typography/lettering, fineline strokes, and boundary-label accuracy — with licensing and autotrace-pollution risk called out per source.

Our situation, restated as the buying criteria: the model is **data-constrained, not capacity-constrained** (val loss 0.00782 → 0.00630 going 100k → 927k images at fixed 4.3M params, zero train/val gap at both scales). So raw volume still buys accuracy. But the deliverable metric is end-to-end mean deltaE on the held-out bench (1.81 no-cleanup → 1.520 best model + vtracer), and the *distribution* gaps — typography, fineline strokes, gradients (excluded by design) — are orthogonal to volume. The census therefore separates **gap-filling fuel** (high value) from **volume-only fuel** (relieves starvation but does not move the gaps) and from **method/reference-only** sources (not mintable, but tell us how to generate or filter).

---

## 1. Executive summary

**The single highest-leverage move is to mint typography ourselves from open fonts, not to scrape more clipart.** Every glyph in an open font is a hand-drawn Bezier outline extractable to SVG with mature zero-cost tooling (fontTools, opentype.js, fontkit, MIT font-to-svg), so it carries effectively zero autotrace-contamination risk, and it directly fills our #1 gap. Scale is ample: Google Fonts (~1,900 families), Noto (195 fonts, 80k glyphs, 162 scripts), Fontsource (~2,000, JSON API), plus a pre-vectorised ~14M-glyph SVG corpus (SVG-Fonts / glyphazzn) that needs no rendering at all. Composing glyphs into wordmarks is well-trodden prior art (MJSynth: ~9M words from a 90k lexicon × 1,400 fonts) and MIT-licensed generators (SynthTIGER) can be forked to emit our exact supervision shape (clean render + per-region label map).

**The biggest hazard is licensing, and one widely-repeated shortcut is wrong.** The verification round *refuted* the claim that Google's `apache/` subset contains Roboto and Open Sans — both were relicensed to OFL and now live under `ofl/`. The `apache/` directory is ~42 mostly novelty/display faces. So the "clean-license" escape hatch is real but small and stylistically narrow. Meanwhile OFL-FAQ Q1.25 (confirmed) treats *font-producing* ML output as virally OFL; we emit non-font SVGs so the letter is narrower than our use, but foundry norms are hostile and rendering-glyphs-to-raster-then-training is an explicitly grey area. For a commercially-sold model this is a decision for a lawyer, not a researcher.

**For the colour/organic gap, flat emoji and CC0 clipart are the clean fuel** — Openclipart (178,604 CC0, already on HuggingFace) is the standout, with autotrace pollution that is *tag-detectable* and therefore filterable rather than fatal. Flat emoji sets (Twemoji CC-BY, Noto Apache-2.0, Fluent MIT) add organic multi-region colour; the new 3D/gradient emoji variants must be excluded to respect the gradients-out constraint.

**For fineline strokes, the stroke-icon families and diagrams/CAD corpora hit the gap directly but raise tracer risk** — they are hairline art, and our ceilings (vtracer 1.28, rust 1.44) were measured on filled icons, so expect worse until validated on hairline slices.

**Two process findings that should gate any multi-source mint:** (1) there is no off-the-shelf autotrace classifier — build cheap structural heuristics (topology, primitive-absence, path/command explosion, palette quantisation, editor-namespace fingerprints); (2) mixing sources in natural ratios reliably produces imbalanced per-domain capability, so hold out per-source *and* per-severity validation slices and optimise the mixture toward deltaE, not raw val loss. Our own single diverged 500k run (train loss rose from epoch 1) is consistent with the missing-gradient-clipping hypothesis and unrelated to data, but it is a reminder to keep per-source val slices so a bad mixture is visible early.

## 2. Findings by sub-topic

Citations are inline. Claims that the adversarial round **refuted** appear here only in corrected form, flagged. Claims it **confirmed** are stated plainly. Where a number was corrected upward/downward, the corrected figure is used.

### 2.1 Open fonts — the typography gap (highest value)

- Font glyphs are **born-vector Bezier outlines** (TrueType quadratic, CFF cubic) extractable directly to SVG path data with mature, zero-cost tooling — fontTools `svgLib.path`, opentype.js `toPathData()`, fontkit, and MIT-licensed [font-to-svg](https://github.com/arthurcloche/font-to-svg) which reads outlines from font tables *rather than autotracing* (**confirmed**). Caveat: font-to-svg does not fully implement composite TrueType glyphs and omits some CFF operators; fontTools/fontkit handle composites, so full coverage exists across the toolset. No autotrace risk because there is no raster→vector step.
- **Licensing is the decisive constraint.** OFL-FAQ Q1.25 states that any *font* produced by an ML system whose training data contains OFL source files is a derivative work that must stay OFL (**confirmed**, verbatim). It is aimed at font-producing models; we emit non-font SVGs, so the letter is narrower than our use, but (a) rendering glyphs to raster and training a cleanup/tracer is a grey area Q1.25 does not address, and (b) the FAQ is SIL guidance, not tested in court. [Source](https://openfontlicense.org/ofl-faq/).
- **CORRECTED (was REFUTED):** The often-repeated claim that Google Fonts' `apache/` subset contains Roboto and Open Sans is **false**. Both were relicensed to the SIL OFL and now live under `ofl/`. The `apache/` directory holds ~42 mostly novelty/display/handwriting families (Permanent Marker, Luckiest Guy, Roboto Slab, Syncopate, etc.). The three-license split (`ofl/`, `apache/`, `ufl/`), the bulk `git clone`, and the per-family `METADATA.pb` + license file are all real, and Apache-2.0 genuinely lacks a viral clause — but the clean-license subset is small and stylistically narrow, **not** a source of the flagship text families. [Source](https://github.com/google/fonts).
- Google Fonts holds ~1,900 families (1,826 as of May 2025), overwhelmingly OFL [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Fonts). Noto is a single OFL family covering 80,000+ characters across 162 scripts in 195 fonts — the widest single-source script diversity, and the best source of non-Latin/complex-stroke shapes [source](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noto_fonts). Fontsource aggregates ~2,000 fonts as npm packages with a free JSON API exposing per-font license — filter to Apache/MIT to sidestep OFL [source](https://github.com/fontsource/font-files).
- **Fontshare** offers ~100 families free for commercial use, but under a mix of ITF's proprietary closed-source Free Font License (FFL) and OFL; the **FFL tier forbids redistribution** and is unsafe as minable feedstock — only the OFL tier is mintable (**confirmed**). [Source](https://www.fontshare.com/licenses/itf-ffl).
- A pre-vectorised born-vector glyph corpus already exists: **SVG-Fonts / glyphazzn** (SVG-VAE, Google Magenta), ~14M font characters normalised to SVG paths — minable directly with no font rendering [source](https://magenta.withgoogle.com/svg-vae). Paths are normalised, so fidelity may not match raw outlines.
- **Composing glyphs into wordmarks is high-yield prior art.** CORRECTED: MJSynth (Jaderberg et al. 2014) rendered ~9M cropped word images from a 90k-word English lexicon × 1,400+ Google Fonts via font-render → border/shadow → base-color → projective-distortion → natural-blending → noise. Correct citation is [arXiv:1406.2227](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2227) and the [VGG data page](https://www.robots.ox.ac.uk/~vgg/data/text/), **not** github.com/ankush-me/SynthText (that repo is the separate Gupta et al. 2016 scene-compositing SynthText). The transferable core for us is the glyph-layout front end, not the scene-blend back end. **SynthTIGER** (MIT, Clova AI) is the best off-the-shelf generator to fork for minting wordmark pairs [source](https://github.com/clovaai/synthtiger).
- **CORRECTED (was REFUTED):** The claim of a documented "50k+ text-logo pipeline with per-glyph binary masks + bboxes and affine/skew/perspective augmentation" is **not supported** by its cited source. arXiv:2512.20479 (UTDesign) is a diffusion-transformer text-editing framework; it renders ~28.8M *individual character* images from 4,194 TTF fonts × 6,857 chars with automated glyph-level bounding boxes (YOLOv11n) and RGBA foreground extraction, and augments with Gaussian blur / downsample / noise / background change. It contains no layout-template logo composition, no 50k-logo dataset, and no per-glyph binary-mask/affine pipeline; those specifics appear misattributed. What is genuinely supported: font-driven per-character glyph synthesis with automated glyph-level bbox + foreground labels — still useful, and the blur/downsample/noise augmentation matches our Stage-1 corruption family. [Source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.20479).

### 2.2 Emoji and icon families — colour and fineline gaps

- Flat emoji are flat solid-color multi-region organic-curve shapes with no typography — an under-represented distribution that fits the gradients-out constraint **when limited to flat variants**. **Exclude** Google's Noto 3D (July 2026) and Fluent 3D/Color: they are true-3D/gradient and violate the constraint [source](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/platforms/android/world-emoji-day-noto-3d/).
- Licensing splits matter for a commercial model: **OpenMoji is CC-BY-SA 4.0 (share-alike)** — a genuine copyleft contamination risk, unlike the permissive alternatives (**confirmed**; current set size ~4,495, corrected up from ~4,083) [source](https://openmoji.org/faq/). **Twemoji** graphics are CC-BY 4.0 (attribution), code MIT; CORRECTED count is ~4,009 SVGs in the maintained jdecked fork (not ~3,245), Unicode 17.0 [source](https://github.com/jdecked/twemoji). **Noto Emoji** images are Apache-2.0 (fonts OFL), ~3,700 glyphs [source](https://github.com/googlefonts/noto-emoji). **Fluent Emoji** is MIT — mint Flat + High-Contrast only [source](https://github.com/microsoft/fluentui-emoji).
- Stroke icon families are 2px-stroke on 24×24 grids, directly addressing the **fineline gap**, nearly all MIT/ISC/Apache: Feather 287 (MIT), Lucide ~1,640 (ISC), Tabler ~6,166 (MIT), Iconoir ~1,600 (MIT), Phosphor ~1,500 base × 6 weights ≈ 9,000 (MIT), Material Symbols ~3,300 glyphs × fills/weights/optical-sizes (Apache-2.0), Font Awesome Free ~2,000 (CC-BY, Brands are trademarks), **Microsoft Fluent System Icons ~18,600 mono + ~857 color (MIT)** — the single biggest clean icon supply found [source](https://github.com/microsoft/fluentui-system-icons). Phosphor's 6 weights and Tabler's adjustable stroke-width give built-in stroke augmentation.

### 2.3 Clipart, aggregators and the illustration/colour gap

- **Openclipart** (nyuuzyou HF mirror) is the standout for the illustration-vs-icon gap: **178,604 fully-CC0, full-colour illustration SVGs**, 22.3 GB, already minified and tagged, with autotrace pollution that is **tag-detectable** (`filter autotrace`, `vectorized`, `upload2openclipart`) so it can be filtered rather than discarded [source](https://huggingface.co/datasets/nyuuzyou/openclipart).
- HuggingFace user **nyuuzyou** has packaged the canonical clipart/icon web into four ready-to-mine corpora with embedded raw SVG that dwarf svg-stack: **svgfind 3,655,810** (CC-BY), **clker ~255,758** (CC0), **svgrepo ~218,000** (12-way license mix incl. GPL 11k and CC-BY-NC 135), **openclipart 178,604** (CC0). These relieve data-starvation immediately but are **icons/symbols/clipart — the domain the corpus already covers — so they add volume, not gap coverage** [source](https://huggingface.co/datasets/nyuuzyou/svgfind).
- **Heavy duplication trap:** freesvg.org (170k CC0) and publicdomainvectors.org share administrators, and freesvg re-hosts ~55k Openclipart images — dedup all three before minting [source](https://freesvg.org/pages/about-us). **clker is high autotrace-risk** (historically an autotrace-from-raster site) [source](https://huggingface.co/datasets/nyuuzyou/clker-svg).
- **FIGR-8-SVG** (1,548,256 monochrome icons) is MIT-*wrapped* but its images come from The Noun Project with commercial-use restrictions — risky for a commercial vectorizer [source](https://github.com/marcdemers/FIGR-8). **starvector/FIGR-SVG** (1.33M) is, despite the name, single-path icons (FIGR-8-derived), overlapping existing icon bias [source](https://huggingface.co/datasets/starvector/FIGR-SVG).
- **Wikimedia Commons** has **no public media dumps since ~2013** — mine via the MediaWiki API (`allimages mimetype=image/svg+xml`, `categorymembers`) or tools like comload (GPLv3); it is a per-file license mix (CC0/CC-BY/CC-BY-SA/GFDL/PD) that must be read per file, and Potrace-traced files are template-tagged (`Created with Potrace`) so filterable [source](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Commons:Dumps_and_backups).
- **Heraldry / coat-of-arms** (Wikimedia + heraldicart.org) is the one niche that attacks the **fineline + high-complexity** gaps: dense hand-built paths, CC0/PD subset available [source](https://heraldicart.org/wikimedia-commons/). **Flags** (emcrisostomo 249, hampusborgos ~250, lipis MIT, plus thousands of historical/subdivision on Wikimedia) are clean flat born-vector but **reinforce existing strength** rather than the gaps. **game-icons.net** (4,180 CC-BY) and NPS/AIGA pictograms are clean but redundant. **Kenney** is PNG-first (skip). **Laser/craft SVG** (3axis, freesvg craft tier) is high autotrace-risk and license-murky (**avoid/heavily filter**). Embroidery/vinyl is a conversion ecosystem, not a corpus (**skip**).

### 2.4 Diagrams, charts, CAD — text-dense fineline (high value, high tracer risk)

- **starvector/svg-diagrams** is the best ready-made diagram corpus: **182,618 born-vector SVGs** (flowcharts, network diagrams, charts, molecular structures, Graphviz, kanji), 379 MB, same org/gating as svg-stack; license not stated on card — verify before commercial use [source](https://huggingface.co/datasets/starvector/svg-diagrams).
- **Beagle** crawled ~41–42k born-vector chart SVGs (~15k Plotly, ~22k Chartblocks, ~2k D3) each paired with a rendered screenshot — matches our cleanup supervision shape — but has no one-click download and unclear redistribution license [source](https://www.cs.tufts.edu/~remco/publications/2018/CHI2018-Beagle.pdf).
- **arXiv figures via pdffigures2 (Apache-2.0) + pdftocairo** can crop figures to vector SVG/EPS, but **born-vector yield is LOW** — one study found only ~11% of sampled figures were vector — so a per-figure born-vector detector (path/text ops vs a single embedded image XObject) is mandatory before minting; high-yield subfields are matplotlib/TikZ/pgfplots-heavy CS/physics [source](https://arxiv.org/abs/1709.02261).
- **Infinite synthetic generators** are zero-license-risk and plug straight into the clean-render→damage pipeline: **Mermaid** (MIT) and **Graphviz** (EPL) render text specs to SVG deterministically, with controllable stroke width to probe the fineline gap [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.14967). **ChartAnchor** ships ~218k Plotly *scripts* (raster as-shipped) re-renderable to SVG [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.01017).
- **CAD/floorplan** are extreme fineline: FloorPlanCAD 15,663 SVG with per-primitive labels (**CC-BY-NC — non-commercial, blocks commercial minting**) [source](https://floorplancad.github.io/); CubiCasa5K 5,000 SVG+raster (CC-BY-NC); ResPlan 17,000 (polygon JSON, permissive, needs SVG conversion); ArchCAD-400K (verify SVG-native vs DXF); the ABC dataset (~1M CAD models) projectable to born-vector 2D. These hit all three gaps at once but the label head and tracer ceilings are untested on hairline art — expect degraded deltaE until validated.

### 2.5 Autotrace detection heuristics (build, don't buy)

There is **no published trained classifier** for autotraced-vs-born-vector; every practitioner uses cheap structural heuristics during curation [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.10894). Directly implementable tells, in rough order of reliability:

1. **Topology** — potrace/Image-Trace line art becomes **closed filled even-odd paths with no stroke**, never centerline strokes. "Filled boundary regions, zero stroked paths" flags tracing [source](https://alpha.inkscape.org/vectors/www.inkscapeforum.com/viewtopic2f2c.html?t=12789).
2. **Primitive absence** — traced output is 100% path-Bezier with **zero `<circle>`/`<rect>`/`<line>`/arc** commands [source](https://potrace.sourceforge.net/potrace.1.html).
3. **Path/command explosion** — literature thresholds: **>8 paths or >32 commands/path** (DeepIcon [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2410.15760)); **>100 Bezier curves/file** (UniSVG [source](https://arxiv.org/html/2508.07766v1)). Segment count *after* RDP/Schneider simplification also separates the two (traced contours resist simplification) [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2007.11301).
4. **Palette quantisation** — multi-color traces are pre-quantised to a small discrete flat palette (8/27/64, or Median-Cut/NeuQuant up to 256) as stacked opaque layers with no gradients [source](https://github.com/migvel/color_trace).
5. **Editor-namespace fingerprints** — born-vector files usually carry `sodipodi:`/`inkscape:`, `sketch:type`, or Illustrator/Figma metadata; raw tracer output is metadata-poor (but SVGO strips these, so absence is weak evidence) [source](https://wiki.inkscape.org/wiki/Inkscape-specific_XML_attributes).

**Tune for precision, not recall:** detection breaks down after post-trace editing, centerline tracing, and on simple geometric shapes — so drop obvious traces and accept false negatives [source](https://alpha.inkscape.org/vectors/www.inkscapeforum.com/viewtopic2f2c.html?t=12789). Named datasets built by vectorising rasters (**MMSVG, InternSVG**, and unfiltered slices of **StarVector svg-stack**) carry high contamination; **DuetSVG's SVG-Hub-1M/5M** already removed auto-vectorised SVGs and is the cleanest born-vector target to mine (license unstated — verify) [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.10894).

### 2.6 Mixture-ratio findings

Mixing sources in natural (availability) ratios reliably produces **imbalanced per-domain capability** — this is the consistent finding across DoReMi, RegMix, FoundIR-v2 and On-Demand Learning [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.21598). Four fixes, cheapest first:

- **Temperature sampling / annealed domain upsampling** — one-line rebalancers; upsample under-represented sources (typography, fineline), especially in the **last 10–20% of training**, which buys large targeted gains at small general cost [source](https://arxiv.org/html/2406.03476).
- **Online loss-based reweighting** — ODM's Exp3 bandit on per-domain loss (~19% fewer iterations, near-free) [source](https://arxiv.org/html/2312.02406v2); FoundIR-v2's Data Equilibrium Scheduling (softmax on recent per-task performance change) is the closest precedent — it is literally mixture optimisation for a multi-degradation restoration model [source](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.09282).
- **Offline predict-then-train mixing laws** — RegMix (many tiny parallel proxies + regression) is the most practical [source](https://arxiv.org/html/2407.01492v1); data-mixing-law/BiMix give <0.2% loss-extrapolation error [source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14908); DoReMi works but is **fragile and non-transferable** across arch/tokenizer, so RegMix/DOGE are safer [source](https://github.com/sangmichaelxie/doremi).
- **Difficulty curricula** — On-Demand Learning names the exact "fixation" failure (a restorer trained at one corruption level fails at others) and fixes it by binning degradation into difficulty sub-tasks with adaptive re-allocation — directly applicable to our JPEG-quality/noise-sigma/blur-radius axes [source](https://vision.cs.utexas.edu/projects/on_demand_learning/).

**Two mechanics specific to us:** optimise the mixture toward **end-to-end deltaE, not raw UNet val loss** (RegMix: "choosing the correct target is crucial" [source](https://github.com/sail-sg/regmix)); and under batch-16 SGD, **upsampling ≠ loss-upweighting** — upsampling gives lower gradient variance (faster, more overfitting), so "Cooldown" tapers it over training [source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.04579). Hold out **per-source and per-severity** validation slices, not one global set.

### 2.7 Degradation realism

Our JPEG+noise+blur wreck is a **first-order, fixed-order, single-codec** model, and the restoration literature is unanimous this under-covers real inputs. Highest-leverage upgrades:

- **Second-order degradation** — apply the whole chain (blur→resize→noise→JPEG) **twice** with independent params (Real-ESRGAN) [source](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2107.10833).
- **Sinc ringing/overshoot** — ~80% probability at the final step; vector icons are all hard edges, so edge ringing is arguably the single most relevant artifact we currently omit [same source].
- **Randomise operation order** — BSRGAN's central finding; fixed-order under-covers regardless of range tuning [source](https://github.com/cszn/BSRGAN).
- **Modern codecs** — WebP/AVIF/H.264/H.265 now dominate web/screenshot inputs, not JPEG; APISR's compression-probability table is copyable [source](https://arxiv.org/html/2403.01598v2). APISR also shows **compression thins/breaks thin lines** (our fineline gap) and counters it by **sharpening the ground truth** (recursive USM + XDoG) so the model learns to restore, not just denoise.
- **Double/non-aligned JPEG** — real customer files are recompressed; FBCNN shows non-aligned 8×8 blocks (QF1≤QF2) break blind restorers unless trained on them [source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2109.14573).
- **Nearest-neighbor resize** for screen/gaming content; **random up+down resize** with mixed interpolation for upsampled rasters [Real-ESRGAN source].
- **Scan class** (zero current coverage): halftone/descreen, moire, bleed-through, global color shift, paper texture, laser/crumple/dust — the Descanning taxonomy; **DESCAN-18K source imagery is CC-BY-NC-SA (non-commercial)**, so use it as a realism reference and replicate its synthetic recipe rather than mint from it [source](https://github.com/jhcha08/Descanning).
- **Judge cleanup on what breaks the trace**, not deltaE alone — Scan2CAD practitioner guidance: anti-alias region explosion, speckle→stray marks, broken/thin lines, edge-touching shapes [source](https://scan2cad.com/tips/raster-effects).
- **Strategic:** once the hand-tuned wreck plateaus, AnimeSR shows learned degradation operators mined from real customer files can close the remaining synth-vs-real gap [source](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2206.07038). Deep Vectorization's paper-background compositing (render primitives onto 40 real paper scans + blur/noise) is a cheap degradation we omit [source](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2003.05471).

## 5. Open questions the round could not settle

1. **Does OFL Q1.25 bind a raster-cleanup/tracer trained on rendered OFL glyphs?** Q1.25 is confirmed but explicitly targets *font-producing* systems; the render-to-raster-then-train case is a grey area no source addresses and no court has tested. Needs legal opinion, not more research.
2. **What is the actual born-vector yield of an arXiv figure crawl?** The only datapoint is ~11% from one small study; the true rate for matplotlib/TikZ-heavy CS/physics is unknown and determines whether pdffigures2 mining is worth the pipeline. Cheap to measure: sample 500 figures, run a path/text-vs-image-XObject detector.
3. **How much do our tracer ceilings (vtracer 1.28, rust 1.44) degrade on hairline/CAD art?** Measured only on filled icons; the fineline sources may trace far worse, which would change their value ranking. Needs a dedicated hairline eval slice.
4. **Is Beagle (~42k paired chart SVGs) still obtainable, and under what license?** No one-click download and no clear redistribution license were found — requires author contact.
5. **Actual size/license of DuetSVG SVG-Hub-1M/5M and starvector/svg-diagrams.** Both are the cleanest/most-relevant ready-made corpora but ship with unstated licenses ("to-be-released" / not on card) — must verify before any commercial mint.
6. **Real customer-file degradation distribution.** We are inferring the codec/scan mix from the SR literature; a small labelled sample of actual print-shop customer uploads would let us weight the wreck correctly (and later enable AnimeSR-style learned operators).
7. **Whether mixing-law proxy runs transfer at our scale.** RegMix/BiMix evidence is from LLMs; the ~1M-image image-restoration regime may behave differently. A handful of tiny proxy runs would confirm before committing to a 5M mint.
