Model size mattered less than the engine

Rerunning the relay gauntlet with the fresh 10k weights improved raw cleanup by 16% (0.66 to 0.55) but moved the final SVG error by only 1–4%. Once the input to the tracer is nearly clean, the tracer's own approximation dominates what remains. Two honest gaps to close before treating that as gospel: we have not yet measured the engine's error floor on perfect input (the ceiling test), and mean dE hides local geometry failures that a p99 or a boundary-aware metric would catch.

What this buys you: the roadmap decision you wanted from the scaling sweep arrived by a cheaper route. Capacity and data are not the constraint at this scale; the vectorization stage is. That points the next sprint at the output-head question (feeding the engine label maps instead of RGB) and at engine-side quality, before any larger model is worth its training bill.
